Home Sweet Home Comfort
Simple Tips to Reduce the Allergen-Causing Irritants in Your Home

MEMPHIS, Tenn. (August 12, 2004) - Airborne allergens respect no boundaries. They float on the breeze in your home from room to room, causing chronic cold-like symptoms, including runny nose, nasal stuffiness, sneezing, throat clearing, coughing, wheezing, difficulty breathing and itchy or watery eyes. Dust, mold, pollen and pet dander are just a few of the allergy-causing irritants commonly found in homes.

“Far too often, people mistake wintertime allergies for a cold or flu,” says Art Massey, vice president of marketing and product development for the home comfort division of Hunter Fan Co. “Starting in the fall, we tend to have less fresh air circulating in our homes, which can result in the onset of pronounced allergy symptoms.”

To keep allergy-causing irritants at bay, follow these tips:
  1. Use allergy-reducing filters while heating and cooling your home.
  2. Vacuum at least twice a week, using HEPA filter bags.
  3. Encase your pillows and mattresses in allergen-proof covers. Bedding, like upholstery and clothing, is a perfect breeding ground for dust mites.
  4. Wash bedding once a week in HOT water.
  5. Wear an air-filtering mask when cleaning your pet’s litter pan, tanks or cages.
  6. Use blinds or shades that can be cleaned regularly instead of large fabric drapes that can trap and accumulate dust.
  7. Switch to silk houseplants. Live houseplants produce pollens and encourage mold growth.
  8. Take a look at your child’s toys. Toys made, trimmed or stuffed with animal hair or seeds (beanbags) may be a source of allergens.
  9. Keep the moisture level in your home below 50 percent. Dust mites live off moisture. Try to keep plants and fish tanks out of bedrooms, as they increase the moisture level.
  10. Frequently clean the refrigerator by throwing away moldy food items, and wipe with bleach to discourage mold growth.

According to The Asthma and Allergy Foundation of America, at least one out of every five Americans suffers from allergic diseases–the sixth leading cause of chronic disease in the United States. This reinforces the need to protect your home and family from airborne allergens. Investing in a CADR certified high-efficiency air purifier that is 99.97 percent effective in removing airborne particles is the first and best line of defense.

Clean Air Delivery Rate (CADR) is the industry-standard rating system set by the Association of Home Appliance Manufacturers (AHAM). AHAM requires independent testing and certification of air purifier performance and effectiveness by measuring the amount of clean air that the unit delivers into a room.
