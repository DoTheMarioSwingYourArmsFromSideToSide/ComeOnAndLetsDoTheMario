HUNTER FAN CO. REVEALS THE ELEGANTLY BOLD CHARMAINE™

MEMPHIS, Tenn. (January 17, 2003) — Hunter Fan Co. unveils the Charmaine™— a striking new ceiling fan poised for an individually styled living space with ingenious amenities that enhance the enjoyment of a comfortably cool room.

The Charmaine’s crowning glory is an eye-catching integrated uplight. Four separately sold 60-watt bulbs bathe the luxurious marble-like faux alabaster bowl in light as it sits in a richly detailed die-cast motor housing. Three spindle arms extend from the ornamented ceiling mount to support the uplight at its rim. A removable downlight lit with a separately sold single 60-watt bulb is also designed in faux alabaster and accented by the same impeccably styled motor housing.

Five 54-inch removable dual-sided blades reach out from the housing in dark walnut and mahogany veneer with antique brass finish or dark walnut and dark cherry veneer with antique pewter finish.

“A fan’s quality is expressed in both its form and function. Hunter’s Charmaine is a superb example in both categories,” said Jim Barrett, vice president for Hunter’s Ceiling Fan Division.

All of Charmaine’s lighting and fan speed options can be commanded from anywhere in the room by a full-function remote control. Perfect fan balance and easy installation are part of this fan’s package with Hunter’s unique WobbleFree™ Canopy mounting system. A hands-free loop temporarily supports the fan during wiring, and its larger ceiling plate design provides greater installation flexibility. A self-aligning trilobular ball maintains full, even contact with the canopy housing, eliminating stress points that often contribute to wobble.
