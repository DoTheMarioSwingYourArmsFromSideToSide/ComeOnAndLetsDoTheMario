Pagosa Springs Interpretive Alliance

 


Artwork by Ashley Snyder,
Pagosa Springs High School

 

The Interpretive Alliance consists of several organizations, agencies, and individuals who have combined efforts to interpret and enhance public interest in and appreciation of natural and cultural resources.

The calendar [PDF, 1 page, 120 KB] lists events related to the natural and cultural resources of Pagosa Springs and surrounding areas. Children are welcome to all our programs.

In addition to the calendars, events will be listed in "Kate’s Calendar" in the Pagosa Springs Sun and on the Community Calendar on KWUF Radio. All programs are free.

Exhibits

  • August 4 through 31
    2nd Annual Juried Art Exhibit of works of local artists
    A reception will be held August 4 from 5-7 p.m.
    Location: Wild Spirit Gallery
    Sponsor: Pagosa Springs Arts Council, 970-264-5020
    August 4-7: See you at the Archuleta County Fair

  • Navajo State Park Visitor Center provides interpretive and educational displays all year. Please contact the park at 883-2208 for information. There is a park entrance fee. Programs are free.

  • The Southern Ute Indian Cultural Center and Museum brings to life the rich history of the Ute Indian People. (970) 563-9583 for more information.

  • At the Colorado Division of Wildlife Visitor Center in Durango, you will find an impressive and interesting display of wildlife and wildlife habitat. The visitor center is open daily at 151 E. 16th Street. Contact the center for hours and additional information at (970) 247-0855.

  • Visit the Pagosa Springs Arts Council Gallery and enjoy shows of local artists at Town Park on Hermosa Street in Pagosa Springs.
