Colorado Fall Vacation in Pagosa Springs

Soak in nature’s incredible palette during the Fall in Pagosa Springs. Witness various shades of pine and spruce, blankets of golden aspen groves, glistening blue skies, specks of wildflowers, deep blue river swirls, glimmering emerald lakes, and shining black volcanic and granite rocks jutting through the foliage.  Pagosa Springs will delight your senses during our warm autumn days.  Whether you are:

  • Hiking into our 3 million acres of Colorado wilderness (View Hiking Map)
  • Fly fishing and hooking your first rainbow trout (View Fishing Brochure)
  • 4-wheeling or mountain biking (View Mountain Biking/4-Wheeling Outfitters)
  • Colorado elk hunting or deer hunting along with pack trips into National Forest (View Hunting Outfitters)
  • Playing our 18-Hole Golf Course (View http://www.golfpagosa.com) or
  • Driving through miles of scenic vistas and incredible fall colors (View Driving Brochure)

In the winter, it’s all about the snow. With the “most snow in Colorado”, we don’t have to make it or wait for it very long. It comes…all natural 465+ feet of it at the summit. If you’re looking to make virgin tracks, ski into the largest backcountry in the nation, or simply enjoy our sledding hills in town, Pagosa Springs is your winter destination.
