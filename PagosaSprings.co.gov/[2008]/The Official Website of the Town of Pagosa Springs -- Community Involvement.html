Community Involvement

Neighborhood Watch Program

"Small communities grow great through harmony, great ones fall to pieces through discord." Sallust (86 BC - 34 BC)

Once again we are attempting to round up a number of community organizers to create a town Neighborhood Watch Program.  Neighborhood Watch is a national program that encourages neighborhoods to work cooperatively with the Police Department to reduce crime and promote a safer community.  The Pagosa Springs Police Department will do everything it can to assist you in developing your Neighborhood Watch Programs, but it can only be successful with active participants and motivated leaders.  If you are a town resident and have questions about coordinating a Neighborhood Watch Program, please contact the department. 

Observer Program

Available to anyone over the age of sixteen, the observer program gives you the opportunity to spend a tour of duty with one of our officers.  The program is designed to provide a better understanding of the police officer’s role in the community.  If you are interested in the observer program, please fill out the application, sign release and read the program rules.  If you have any questions, please contact the department.

 

Hunters Education

The Pagosa Springs Police Department offers hunters education classes periodically throughout the year.  Check our bulletin board for up coming class schedules.

Additional gun safety programs the department hosts include;

  • Women’s firearm safety through Women in the Outdoors.  Dates to be announced
  • Hunter education for sixth graders at Pagosa Springs Intermediate School
  • Hunter Safety class spring and fall

Contact Division of Wildlife in Durango at 247-0855 for other Hunter Safety Classes.

If you are interested in obtaining a concealed weapons permit, contact the Archuleta County Sheriff’s Department at (970) 264-2131.

 

Drug Awareness

The Pagosa Springs Police Department is developing new ways to attack the drug problems and associated crimes related to drugs, and get the community involved in the fight.  Your contribution to the war on drugs includes contacting the Police Department with any information or suspicions about drug activity in your neighborhood.  Call the department at (970) 264-4151 ext. 228 or email us.  

  • Report a Meth Lab and Narcotics Tip Line is a way for you as a citizen to help the police stop the spread of drugs.  By reporting any suspicious or drug related activity anonymously to the police department, we can use these tips to combat drug trafficking, manufacturing, sales, and use.
