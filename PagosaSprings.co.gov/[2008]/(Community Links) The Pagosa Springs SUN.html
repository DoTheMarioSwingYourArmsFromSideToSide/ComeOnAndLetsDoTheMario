Wednesday, April 16, 2008
Summer internships available at LPEA
La Plata Electric Association is seeking incoming or graduating seniors from all high schools (including home-school students) in Archuleta County to participate in a paid summer internship program.

Up to four interns will be selected from Archuleta and La Plata counties, and each will receive an hourly wage.

“We’ve found that students in our area don’t realize the full range of career opportunities that are available at an electric cooperative,” said Linda Looman, the LPEA human resources director who is overseeing the program. “Over the course of the summer, we plan to introduce our interns to possible future career opportunities.”

The LPEA internship program will run from June 9 through Aug. 1, incorporating a four-day work week for the students. During their internship, students will rotate through the various LPEA departments, work with LPEA mentors and be provided with hands-on learning opportunities.

“They won’t be sitting in a corner,” assured Looman. “The program will be flexible, and as the program progresses and we determine their skills and interests, we will nurture those in the students, perhaps focusing in specific areas as appropriate.”

The LPEA program will also be tailored to a student’s summer schedule — for example, a planned family vacation or athletic training. The intent is, according to Looman, to make it a win-win for students and the cooperative.

Applications are available on the LPEA Web site, www.lpea.coop, and questions can be directed to Looman at 382-3572. Deadline for application is May 1.

Friday, April 11, 2008
Test Tornado Warnings, Tuesday
On Tuesday morning, April 15, the National Weather Service (NOAA) will broadcast test tornado warnings over NOAA weather radio transmitters throughout Colorado. The exact time the mock warnings will be issued has not been determined, but they are expected sometime between 8:45 and 9:45 a.m.

The test warnings will also serve to activate the Emergency Alert System across the state and, according to NOAA spokesman Jim Pringle, most western Colorado radio and television engineers are aware of the planned trial.

In order to test its short-fused warning communications, NOAA annually conducts the operation in accordance with Severe Weather Awareness Week. In Colorado, this year’s dates are April 13 through April 19.

Anyone realizing problems or concerns with the April 15 test warnings is asked to contact NOAA Meteorologist Jim Pringle at 970-243-7007, extension 726, or e-mail him at james.pringle@noaa.gov.

Thursday, April 10, 2008
Suspects held in area robberies
By James Robinson

Staff Writer

Two suspects possibly linked to a string of area bank robberies, including one at the First Southwest Bank in Pagosa Springs April 1, are in custody, according to an FBI spokesperson.

Steve Marshall, public information officer for the Albuquerque division of the FBI said 32-year-old Kacey Fisher of Bloomfield, N.M. and his alleged accomplice, 31-year-old Julia Norris of the Farmington, N.M. area are both being held in the San Juan County, N.M jail without bond. Both face a number of federal charges.

Fisher is being held on a state parole violation and will likely face federal bank robbery charges.

Investigators say Fisher could be linked to three robberies in northern Arizona earlier in the year, and three others — one in Durango March 26, another in Farmington and the April 1 incident in Pagosa Springs.

Norris faces three federal charges of aiding and abetting in a bank robbery.

Marshall said Fisher was arrested April 4. According to Marshall, Fisher barricaded himself in a Farmington hotel room, but ultimately surrendered after talking to local and federal authorities.

Marshall would not disclose the amounts of money taken in any of the incidents, although local officials reported $2,000 stolen in the Pagosa Springs robbery.

Wednesday, April 9, 2008
Elections bring out voters, some change
Twenty-two percent of registered Pagosa Springs voters cast their votes Tuesday, unseating two out of three incumbents in the race.

In the 6-way race for town council, the big winner was Shari Pierce with 122 votes (61.6%), followed closely by former council member Jerry Jackson, who took 115 votes (58.0%). Incumbent Stan Holt held his council seat with 98 votes (49.5%).

Two other town council incumbents did not fare as well: Tony Simmons lost with 42.4% of the vote (84 votes) while John Steinert was unseated with 35.9% (71 votes). Challenger and first-time candidate Bobby Hart placed last, with 30.3% (60 votes).

Wednesday, April 2, 2008
Search continues for bank robber
The search continues for a gunman who robbed a Pagosa Springs Bank Tuesday afternoon.

The gunman entered First Southwest Bank at 7th and San Juan Streets in downtown Pagosa Springs at approximately 12:30 p.m. Tuesday and robbed the bank of an estimated $2,000 (in 20, 50 and 100-dollar denominations). No one was hurt in the robbery.

A 911 call reported the robbery at approximately 12:30 p.m. A call was apparently made prior to the robbery report, alerting authorities to a suspicious person at the bank.

The suspect left the scene in a full-size, late-model (2000s) metallic bronze or champagne-colored pickup truck, which witnesses said had New Mexico license plates. The suspect reportedly drove east on San Juan Street (U.S. 160) following the incident.

Witnesses said the suspect is a short, heavy-set, clean-shaven male, approximately 5-foot, 5 inches to 5-6 in height. He was wearing a maroon jacket with white stripes on the sleeve and a tan ball cap, with a brown bill. A similarly described suspect robbed a Durango bank last week.

Town assistant police chief Jim Saunders was at the scene within two minutes and local law enforcement officers — town police, sheriff deputies and Colorado State Patrol — began a methodical search of the highways leading into and out of town.

Efforts were hampered by a widespread law enforcement computer system malfunction that affected communication between agencies throughout the region.

Pagosa Springs police detective Scott Maxwell said Wednesday morning that the local department is working with the FBI and the Durango Police Department concerning the robbery and its possible connection to the Durango crime. He said calls have been made to the Farmington Police Department to determine possible links with recent bank robberies there.

Wednesday, March 26, 2008

Forest Service plans prescribed burns
By Phyllis Wheaton

Special to The SUN

Fire managers at Pagosa Ranger District/Field Office of the San Juan Public Lands are planning to conduct prescribed burns on National Forest and Bureau of Land Management Lands during the spring.

Conditions allowing, burning could begin Thursday or Friday in Burns Canyon, nine miles southwest of Pagosa Springs. Smoke will likely be visible from downtown Pagosa Springs, along U.S. 84 south of town, and from the Pagosa Lakes area.

Firefighters will be burning slash piles which are residue from thinning projects completed last fall.

Areas identified for burning this spring include:

• Bureau of Land Management lands on Vigil and Abeyta Mesas, 25 miles south of Pagosa Springs.

• Burns Canyon.

• 225 acres in the Lower Middle Mountain area about 12 miles west of Pagosa Springs.

• The headwaters of Devil Creek in the Turkey Springs area seven miles northwest of downtown of Pagosa Springs.

• Within the Piedra Area near the end of First Fork Road, on the west side of the Piedra River, between Sand Creek and First Fork.

For additional information about these burns or other fuels-reduction efforts, visit the local San Juan Public Lands office, Pagosa Ranger District, at 180 Pagosa Street, Pagosa Springs or call 264-2268.

Tuesday, March 25, 2008
Business input will help shape Colorado aviation
The Colorado Department of Transportation’s (CDOT) Division of Aeronautics is conducting an online survey seeking business input on airport usage. Specifically, CDOT is interested in assessing business dependence on general aviation, commercial aviation and air cargo services as well as gaining information on what factors are important when businesses consider expansion or relocation. This input will be used to shape the future of Colorado’s airports to better meet the needs of the business community. 

A prior study conducted by CDOT’s Aeronautics Division estimated that there are at least 13,000 non-aviation jobs in the state that are dependent on the commercial and general aviation airports.

The survey can be accessed at the following Web address:  www.wilbursmith.com/colorado_impact_study/.

The survey can also be accessed by visiting www.colorado-aeronautics.org and clicking on the “Business Survey” icon in the center of the page.

Wednesday, March 19, 2008
Williams Creek Road closed to motorized, wheeled vehicles
Effective March 17, Williams Creek Road (National Forest System Road No. 640) is closed to motorized, wheeled vehicles due to spring conditions.

Roads are seasonally closed to motorized, wheeled vehicles (including ATVs) to protect road surfaces and for public safety. They are open to non-motorized uses, including bicycling, hiking, and horseback riding.

As with all areas on the forest, damage to roads is not allowed. Do not leave road surfaces where the ground is wet.

Some seniors, disabled vets qualify for property tax exemptions
The Colorado Constitution establishes a property tax exemption for senior citizens, surviving spouses of senior citizens, and for disabled veterans.

For those who qualify, 50 percent of the first $200,000 in actual value of their primary residence is exempted, for a maximum exemption amount of $100,000 in actual value. The State of Colorado pays the property taxes on the exempted value.

Tuesday, March 18, 2008

Audubon Society to hold meeting, birding field trip
The Weminuche Audubon Society will hold two interesting and informative events this week, and the general public is encouraged to attend.

Thursday evening at 6 p.m., just hours before the official start of spring, the group will meet at the First Southwest Bank Conference Room, 249 Navajo Trail Dr., Pagosa Springs. The multifaceted affair will include a discussion of past and future society activities, an open nomination of candidates for the society board of directors and a fascinating presentation featuring three associates of the Durango Wildlife Rehabilitation Center (DWRC).

As the fledgling society works to become an official Audubon chapter, anyone interested in assisting with its formation and development is asked to step forward during the nominating process. According to Audubon Colorado Southwest Regional Director Linda Newberry, willing and devoted leaders are desperately needed to develop an effective organization able to carry out the society mission.

Once society affairs are put to rest, Carole Withers, Tara Bodine, and Dr. Charles Hawman will discuss “Caring for Wildlife at the Durango Wildlife Rehabilitation Center.” Withers is DWRC vice president and a raptor rehabilitation specialist, Bodine is a registered veterinarian technician and licensed wildlife rehabilitator, and Hawman is a doctor of veterinary medicine at the Durango Animal Hospital, and serves as medical director for the DWRC.

The DWRC began rehabilitating ill, injured, orphaned and imprinted (on humans) wildlife in 1998. As a 501(c)(3) non-profit, it cares for birds of prey, migratory songbirds, large game and small mammals until ready for release back into the wild.

On Saturday, March 22, just two days after the meeting, all interested citizens are welcome to participate in a “spring birding” field trip along Colo. 151. Attendees should meet in the Pagosa Springs Alco parking lot at 8 a.m., or at the intersection of Colo. 151 and U.S. 160 at 8:30.

To assure safety, the group will carpool to various stopping points and observe upland birds and waterfowl in their natural habitat. Stops will include areas along the Piedra River, various side creeks, a state wildlife viewing area and critical wetlands near Allison. Participants should dress for outdoor winter activities and bring binoculars, field guides, a lunch and water.

For more information on these two events, call Linda Newberry at (970) 884-8555 or 946-7936. If adverse weather persists the morning of the field trip, call by 7 a.m. to verify whether the outing will take place as planned.

Friday, March 14, 2008
ACHS food drive benefits community
Archuleta County High School (ACHS) studentsare once again be “pounding the pavement” to conduct a food drive for Pagosa residents in need.

This food drive is done in collaboration with the Community United Methodist Church and both City Markets.

Students will distribute bags donated by City Market to different neighborhoods than those covered during the November 2007 food drive. Recipients of the bags are asked to fill the bags with non-perishable food items and to place filled bags on their doorsteps on Thursday, March 20, so that ACHS students can collect the bags and deliver the food items to the Community United Methodist Church. The CUMC food pantry has seen an increase in requests, and is in need of restocking.

The church was selected to be the beneficiary of this food drive because of its “no criteria” policy of giving food to anyone asks for it, or as Pastor Don Ford says, “ask and you shall receive.” Several ACHS families have used this service, and the church donates space to ACHS. The students wanted to do something in return for the church’s generosity as part of their service learning program.

ACHS has a well-developed service learning program. All students participate in a monthly all-school service day working at Loaves and Fishes, completing miscellaneous service projects, or running the school’s EPYCS (El Pomar Youth in Community Service) program. The school also has a required credit of service learning for all students. The course requires students to complete a semester-long project and an academic component that focuses on community issues and citizenship skills. The multiple aspects of the program allow for students to benefit based on their individual needs and interests.
