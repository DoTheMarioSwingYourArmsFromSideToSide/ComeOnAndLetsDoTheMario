    Fire Weather Warning

    Statement as of 12:41 PM MDT on May 09, 2008


    ... Red flag warning in effect until 8 PM MDT this evening for
    southwest Colorado fire zone 207 below 7000 feet elevation for gusty
    winds and low relative humidity...

    The National Weather Service in Grand Junction has issued a red
    flag warning until 8 PM MDT this evening for southwest Colorado
    fire zone 207 below 7000 feet elevation.

    Gusty winds will continue to develop across southwest Colorado
    this afternoon as a weak upper disturbance combines with daytime
    heating to mix down stronger winds aloft. Afternoon mixing will
    also bring down dry air aloft... helping to lower relative
    humidity as well. After 2 PM... wind gusts near 30 mph and relative
    humidity of 10 to 15 percent are expected below 7000 feet
    elevation... where fuels are the driest.

    A red flag warning means that critical fire weather conditions are
    either occurring now... or will shortly. A combination of gusty winds...
    low relative humidity will create explosive fire growth potential.


    Jad
