Welcome
Mark your calendar for our 22nd season July 11 - August 3, 2008. Join us for orchestra, chamber music and special concerts featuring more than 250 musicians under the leadership of Artistic Director Gregory Hustis plus performances by young artists in conjunction with Conservatory Music in the Mountains led by Arkady Fomin. Welcome new Music Director and Conductor Guillermo Figueroa in his inaugural season with the Festival. Concerts are held in multiple venues, including Durango Mountain Resort; Fort Lewis College; Indian Shadow; BootJack Ranch in Pagosa Springs and community parks against the backdrop of the spectacular San Juan Mountains.

About the Festival
Beginning in 1987 with 11 musicians, Music in the Mountains has grown to a three-week festival featuring musicians from around the world and offering orchestral, chamber, conservatory, and Pops concerts. Free performances for children and their families are featured during the Festival.

Mission Statement:
The mission of Music in the Mountains is to provide quality classical music performances for residents of the Four Corners and visitors, with a special focus on introducing classical music to young people.

2008 Festival Artwork

"Follow the Music" is an authentic original weaving by Burnham-style Navajo rug maker Helen Begay. Like her Begay sisters, Helen has created a new weaving aesthetic that uses natural hand-spun churro sheep wool and natural dyes made from local plants, minerals, tea, walnuts and even potpourri to create rich earth tones. Her woven narrative painting, which depticts the legendary Kokopelli leading a troupe of musicians across a Southwest frontier, will be auctioned at Pops Night in Durango July 26.
