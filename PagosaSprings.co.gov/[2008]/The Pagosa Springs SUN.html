Wednesday, January 9, 2008
Audubon chapter to meet next week
Identifying animal and bird tracks in the snow can be a daunting task. Therefore, Audubon Colorado and the Jake and Terry Hershey Audubon Outreach Program invite all to attend the next Weminuche Audubon Chapter meeting Wednesday, Jan. 17, at 6 p.m. This informative gathering will be at the First Southwest Bank conference room, 249 Navajo Trail Drive.

After briefly catching up on recent and future chapter activities, attendees will enjoy an informal presentation by Skip Fischer and LYll Willmarth. Fisher is a wildlife biologist for the Columbine Ranger District of the United States Forest Service, and Willmarth is a tracker with experience in the Colorado Division of Wildlife Lynx Reintroduction program.

Because birding is more challenging and somewhat limited over the winter months, the focus of the presentation will be on trying to observe “whatever species are out there.”

Snowshoeing is typically the most effective means of observation, and allows participants to search for animal and bird tracks after a fresh snowfall. Because searching for tracks is best on the third day after a storm, Fischer and Willmarth will first offer visual aids on what to look for. Then, chapter leaders will set a tentative date for a field trip at the Hershey Ranch, based on the next predicted storm.

Chapter leaders hope you will join them for this interesting program, where everyone will learn about animal and bird tracks. In this area, tracks are likely to include those of elk, deer, mice, turkeys, pine marten, short- and long-tailed weasels, coyotes, snowshoe hare, and cottontails.

If you are from Pagosa Springs, Chromo, Chimney Rock, Ignacio, Allison, Bayfield or Durango, the Weminuche Audubon Chapter is for you. It was created under the Jake and Terry Hershey Outreach Program, and its home base is located at the Hershey Four Mile Ranch, north of Pagosa Springs. The ranch is now available for educational programs and Audubon chapter activities.

For more information on the meeting or other Audubon events, contact Audubon Colorado Southwest Regional Director Linda Newberry at (970) 884-8555, or lnewberry@audubon.org.

Tuesday, January 8, 2008
Search continues today for missing skiers
Search and rescue teams are taking advantage of clear skies and stable weather to ramp up efforts to locate two skiers who were reported missing from Wolf Creek Ski Area Saturday.

According to Mineral County Sheriff Fred Hosselkus, the skiers, 27-year-old Michael George, and 27-year-old Kyle Kerschen, both of Albuquerque, were reported missing Saturday after they failed to show for a meeting with family members Friday, and their car was found in the ski area parking lot Saturday morning.

Hosselkus said the men are believed to have ventured outside the ski area’s boundaries.

According to staff at the Mineral County Sheriff’s Department, search teams ventured out again this morning before first light, and two air searches were launched in conjunction with ground teams searching on snowmobiles, snowboards, or “whatever it takes” to cover the territory.

Staff at the sheriff’s department said teams will cover areas previously searched such as the East Fork and Alberta Lakes areas, in addition to covering new terrain.
As of Tuesday at 8:45 a.m., Mineral County Sheriff’s Department staff reported no leads, and unstable snow and avalanche conditions remain of paramount concern.

Staff from Mineral, Rio Grande, and Archuleta counties, the Forest Service, Upper San Juan Search and Rescue, Wolf Creek Ski area and volunteers from New Mexico are participating in the search.

Monday, January 7, 2008
CDOT updates highway closures
According to the Colorado Department of Transportation (CDOT), as of 1 p.m. today the following mountain passes are closed: U.S. 160 Wolf Creek Pass, U.S. 550 Red Mountain Pass, U.S. 550 Molas Pass, U.S. 550 Coal Bank Pass.

CDOT Maintenance crews and avalanche forecasters from the Colorado Avalanche Information Center continue to work together to mitigate avalanches on these passes, including SH 145, as slide paths continue to load up with new snow.

Wind, very wet snow and low visibility continue to create challenges for the mitigation work. Numerous slides, some exceeding 13 feet high and 1,000 feet long on the roadway, have hit each pass, including slide paths that have either never been seen before or that have not run in over 40 years.

For CDOT road updates, alerts and restrictions go to www.cotrip.org.

Monday, January 7, 2008
Search continues for two missing skiers
Two skiers were reported missing from Wolf Creek Ski Area Saturday. Since then, search and rescue personnel have combed the backcountry but, by this morning, the pair had not been found.

According to Mineral County Sheriff Fred Hosselkus, Michael George and Kyle Kerschen, both 27, and from Albuquerque, were supposed to meet with family members Friday. Hosselkus said the pair never showed.

Saturday morning, ski area staff found the skiers’ car in the ski area parking lot, and the men were officially reported missing.

Hosselkus said a multi-agency search effort is underway, and responders from Mineral, Rio Grande and Archuleta counties, the Forest Service, the ski area and Upper San Juan Search and Rescue are participating.

Hosselkus said searchers are focusing on out-of-bounds locations near the ski area into which skiers often venture.

According to the sheriff, a search crew looked for the men in the Alberta Lake area Sunday.

The sheriff said teams have looked for the skiers on foot, adding that Monday morning two snow machines traveled up the East Fork of the San Juan River and a helicopter search was launched from the ski area. Many ski area drainages terminate in the East Fork area. Hosselkus said the team was searching near the McCarthy property, approximately 12 miles from U.S. 160 near the East Fork Road.

Hosselkus said the helicopter will fly as long as weather permits, although more heavy snow is expected that could curtail the aerial search. Hosselkus said, even with inclement weather, ground crews will remain active.

Heavy weekend snow and more forecast for today, high avalanche danger, the closure of U.S. 160 and communications difficulties have made the search difficult, Hosselkus said.

Monday, January 7, 2008
Crews continue to deal with power outages
 With more snow in the forecast, La Plata Electric Association crews continue to work to restore power to hundreds of customers in Archuleta County affected by the storm that moved through the area beginning Saturday evening, Jan. 5.

 The warm, wet, heavy snow brought down trees and branches throughout the region, many falling on wires, snapping poles and crossarms and disabling the electrical equipment.

 Broken poles have caused outages east of Pagosa along the highway toward Wolf Creek. Approximately 300 customers are affected. Difficulties have arisen because in many locations the lines cross the highway, not allowing for “quick fix” of the poles, but crews are making steady progress.

 Also in Pagosa, LPEA crews have found the faults on CR400 (Four Mile Road) and CR200 (Snowball Road), and are currently working in the area.

 Individual spot outages are being reported throughout the region. LPEA crews are concentrating on the aforementioned major line outages, attending to the smaller faults as they can. Personnel is being rotated for safety and rest, but all are mobilized, plus crews from New Mexico are also assisting in the effort.

 Given the number of outages, phone lines to LPEA have reached maximum capacity, often resulting in repeated busy signals. The cooperative apologizes for any frustration this has caused its customers.
 More information on the systemwide outages will be provided as it comes available.

Computer Fix-It-Free Day Jan. 5
Computer on the blink?

Help could be on the way. The annual Fix-It-Free day is set for Jan. 5.

The mission of the computer Fix-It-Free Day is to provide free technical assistance to members of the community who would otherwise not be able to afford it.

The event benefits those who have a computer by means of hand-me-down or charity that is not functioning correctly. Local computer technicians are donating their time and the Pagosa Springs Community Center is donating space.

Used parts will be provided at no charge for the purpose of repair by the Humane Society and by the volunteering computer technicians. If new parts are needed, they will be provided at a discount.

If you would like to donate parts and/or used computers in advance, label the items “Fix-It-Free” and take them to the Humane Society Thrift Store.

This year, organizers will offer an e-cycling event in conjunction with the Computer Fix-It-Free Day. Archuleta County has offered to provide free recycling for all electronic equipment. If you have dead/old electronic items for recycling, take them to the community center between 9 a.m. and 2 p.m. Jan. 5.

Computer repairs during Fix-It-Free Day are by appointment only. One computer per household will be repaired. Interested individuals can reserve a one-hour time slot by calling 731-6373.

Monday, December 24, 2007
LPEA announces Green Power price reduction
La Plata Electric Association’s board of directors has approved a price reduction for power supplied from renewable resources.

As of Jan. 1, member/customers can opt to pay a premium of $0.80 per 100 kilowatt-hour block. This is down from $1.25 per 100 kilowatt-hour block.

The price reduction comes as a result of Tri-State Generation & Transmission’s revised renewable energy program. Tri-State will now charge its customers, such as LPEA, daily market prices for what it purchases, which currently is approximately $0.40 for each 100 kilowatt-hour block. Because the market price fluctuates and LPEA does not want to continually change the rate to its volunteer green power customers, the LPEA board has opted to add an additional $0.40 to the premium to absorb those market fluctuations, plus to generate funds for local renewable programs and purchase locally produced renewable power.

Currently, Tri-State’s “green power” is generated from wind farms in Wyoming, plus some small hydro and biomass. Though some of these dollars customers spend on green power leave the community, LPEA member/customers who participate in the program are supporting the local cooperative, local renewable purchases, local renewable projects” — as well as local jobs. Hope is that the extra green power dollars generated (above market prices) will help LPEA support more renewable projects in the region, so that in future years more of these energy dollars can remain in the communities served by the cooperative.

Because LPEA’s Green Power purchase program is voluntary, LPEA members must specifically request to purchase blocks. To sign up, visit www.lpea.coop/green_power, or in person at the LPEA office in Pagosa Springs (603. S. 8th St.), or call 247-5786.

Tuesday, December 18, 2007
Clear skies a boon to skywatchers
Today, Mars makes its closest pass by Earth for several years, cruising by our planet at a distance of 55 million miles.

Although not exactly a close brush by human standards, astronomically speaking, 55 million miles marks near peak proximity for the two planets, and the result will cause Mars to appear as one of the brightest objects in our night sky.

To locate Mars, look for a bold, brilliant, orange star-like object high in the northeast near the feet of Gemini, the twins. Observations are best begun after 8 p.m.

Tuesday, December 18, 2007
Weminuche Audubon Chapter meets tomorrow
What kind of duck is that?

Is it a Kestrel or Peregrine?

What is that bird hanging upside down at my feeder?

Which jay has the crest and how do I see and tell the difference?

Learning to identify birds can be daunting. Therefore, Audubon Colorado and the Jake and Terry Hershey Audubon Outreach Program invite the public to attend the next meeting of the Weminuche Audubon Chapter for tips on identifying birds. The meeting is tomorrow, Dec. 19 at 6 p.m. in the conference room of First Southwest Bank, 249 Navajo Trail Drive.

During this third chapter meeting, members and guests will catch up on recent and future group activities. In addition, they will enjoy an informal and fun presentation by Jennifer Kleckner, the Watchable Wildlife volunteer coordinator for the southwest region of the Colorado Division of Wildlife (DOW).

Kleckner will provide information on bird watching and birding by ear, as well as an introduction to field guides and binoculars, thus building on the information the chapter has already reviewed. Kleckner is sure to bring interesting and fun activities useful in teaching basic birding techniques.

Before joining the DOW, Kleckner worked for Durango Nature Studies. As a great teacher, she trained volunteers for the youth education program at the Hershey Four Mile Ranch.

If you’re new to birding, or even an experienced hand, join the Weminuche Audubon Chapter for a warm winter evening of fun and learning. With much to gain, this will help prepare all for next year’s field trips. If you are from Pagosa Springs, Chromo, Chimney Rock, Ignacio, Allison, Bayfield or Durango, this chapter is for you.

The Weminuche Audubon Chapter was created last spring under the Jake and Terry Hershey Outreach Program. Its home base is located at the Hershey Four Mile Ranch, north of Pagosa Springs. The ranch is now available for educational programs and Audubon chapter activities.

For more information on the meeting or other Audubon events, contact Audubon Colorado Southwest Regional Director Linda Newberry at (970) 884-8555 or lnewberry@audubon.org.

Wednesday, December 12, 2007
Wolf Creek Pass closed for avalanche control work
Today, travelers driving east on U.S. 160 may as well take their time heading out of town.

The Colorado Department of Transportation (CDOT) closed the highway between Pagosa Springs and the Wolf Creek Pass summit at 6:30 this morning, to conduct avalanche control.

According to a press release issued by the Pagosa Springs Chamber of Commerce late yesterday, the highway will remain closed until cleanup is complete. However, the Wolf Creek Ski Area Web site suggests CDOT will open the road occasionally — between blasting and clearing snow — to allow traffic to pass in both directions.

Travelers are advised to contact CDOT (877-315-7623) or the Archuleta County Sheriff’s Department (264-5555) for recorded updates, before heading up the pass.



Wednesday, December 12, 2007
County secures $1.09 million grant for bridge repair
More than $1 million in recently awarded grant funding will allow for repairs to Harebell Drive Bridge in San Juan River Village.

According to Archuleta County Special Projects Director Sheila Berger, the bridge has one of the worst sufficiency ratings in county.

The award, said Berger, totals $1.09 million and is for the calendar year 2009.

Initially, the county’s portion of the repair project would have been $274,000, although Berger said the San Juan River Village Metro District will be picking up the county’s share of the tab. Thus, Berger explained, the only costs to the county are administrative costs.

“We are merely working as a third party agent on their behalf,” Berger said. Cynthia Purcell of the metro district, Chris Tanner, interim road and bridge superintendent and Berger worked to secure the grant funding.
The county received notice of the award in a letter dated Dec. 5.

Tuesday, December 11, 2007
LPEA warns of possible power outages today
In an effort to prepare member customers in Archuleta and La Plata counties, La Plata Electric Association officials caution that heavy snow loading the lines could cause varied outages throughout the day today, Dec. 11.

“We’ve gotten word from our field crews that the lines are loading up with snow, especially in the higher elevations of Archuleta County,” said Steve Gregg, LPEA operations manager. “When the snow starts dropping off those lines as the day warms up, that’s when the outages could begin.”

As Gregg explains, electrical lines bow with the snow, and when the snow unloads, those lines “pop” back into place, often interacting with other lines causing fuses to blow. Power is then automatically interrupted.

“That could be the best scenario,” said Gregg. “It’s relatively standard for our linemen to get in and repair a fuse and get folks back in power, as opposed to having damaged wires and equipment. That takes a lot longer to repair.”

Further information on any outages will be relayed as it becomes available.

Friday, December 7, 2007
Lawsuit against county dismissed
A lawsuit that would have placed Archuleta County in federal court to defend against allegations of First Amendment violations has been voluntarily dismissed, according to U.S. District Court documents filed Tuesday.

The plaintiff, La Plata View Holding Company, Inc., doing business as the Pagosa Free Press, filed for a voluntary dismissal Tuesday, with the matter going before Judge Lewis T. Babcock Wednesday.

The lawsuit stemmed from Pagosa Free Press publisher Rick Baker’s allegations that Archuleta County Administrator Bob Campbell had unlawfully blocked e-mail transmissions between county employees and e-mail addresses with the domain name “pagosafreepress.com.” The suit alleges that Campbell instituted the block as retribution for the Free Press’s coverage of Archuleta County affairs.

According to Babcock’s findings, the suit was dismissed without prejudice, with each party ordered to pay its own fees and costs.
Baker said legal fees related to the suit had cost him $2,000.

When asked why he withdrew the suit, Baker said the arduous and expensive prospect of a federal court battle against a battery of attorneys from the county’s insurance company could have pushed his legal tab to $75,000. And, he said, that was a financial burden he wasn’t willing to weather.

Baker said the stakes would have been different if he had sued Campbell personally, but that was a route he didn’t want to take.
“I hoped amongst hope that they (the county) would have unblocked it because it just wasn’t right,” Baker said.

Archuleta County Attorney Teresa Williams said dismissal without prejudice allows the matter to come before the court in the future if the plaintiff so chooses.

In retrospect, Baker said, “I probably should have never filed it.”
