The First Annual B.A.S.H ride took place on May 1st.
Bicyclists From Throughout Texas Descended Upon
Shiner's Spoetzl Brewery

Click on any image to see an enlarged version.

Almost 500 cyclists crossed the finish line at the historic Spoetzl Brewery in Shiner, Texas, completing the first annual Kiwanis International Shiner B.A.S.H. (Bike from Austin, San Antonio and Houston). The 90-mile trek, in honor of the brewery's 90th anniversary, benefited Kiwanis International. The Spoetzl Brewery donated $25,000 to the nonprofit organization that benefits programs for children and seniors.

What better incentive to ride 90 miles than an ice cold Shiner beer? Cyclists and their friends enjoyed plenty of Shiner beer, good food and great music.


Who says the sponsors can't have a good time, too? (But you don't see any of them on bikes, do you?) The first annual Kiwanis International Shiner B.A.S.H. was sponsored by the Spoetzl Brewery and Sun & Ski Sports. Pictured are Bob Devr, Kiwanis International; Alan Kaniss, Kiwanis International; Chuck Varner, Sun & Ski Sports and B.A.S.H. coordinator; Mark Burleson, Kiwanis International; and Charley Mays, Shiner assistant brand manager.

These riders deserve a toast for completing the 90-mile trek to the home of Shiner beer while raising money for a worthy cause. They've earned their bragging rights!

Looks like everyone had a great time after the ride kicking back, enjoying a cold Shiner and listening to some great Texas music.

There's nothing to do now but rest. See ya next year!
