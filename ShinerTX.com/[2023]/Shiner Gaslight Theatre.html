Shiner Gaslight Theatre Presents “Drinking Habits”

Directed By: Melissa Neubauer


Rehearsals are in full swing for “Drinking Habits”, the 2023 summer production at the Shiner Gaslight Dinner Theatre. Accusations, mistaken identities, and romances run wild in this traditional, laugh-out-loud farce. Two nuns at the Sisters of Perpetual Sewing have been secretly making wine to keep the convent's doors open, but Paul and Sally, reporters and former fiancées, are hot on their trail. They go undercover as a nun and priest, but their presence, combined with the addition of a new nun, spurs paranoia throughout the convent that spies have been sent from Rome to shut them down. Wine and secrets are inevitably spilled as everyone tries to preserve the convent and reconnect with lost loves. 

Fri., Sat., and Sun. performances are July 21, 22, 23, 28, 29, 30 and August 4, 5, and 6. A Wed. performance will be July 26.  All are in the evening except for Sunday matinees.  A meal is served before the play.    

Season holder tickets go on sale beginning Wednesday, June 21. General tickets begin Monday, June 26. Please call 361-594-2079 between 8:30 and 4:00 on weekdays to reserve.  All tickets must be reserved in advance.

History

The Shiner Gaslight Theatre is a non-profit community dinner theatre which seats 132 and provides contemporary and classic theatre. Except as noted, plays are appropriate for adults and older children. The Theatre is staffed completely by volunteers.
